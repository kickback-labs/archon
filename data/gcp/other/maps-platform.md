## GCP Google Maps Platform

### Description
Google Maps Platform is a suite of APIs and SDKs that gives developers programmatic access to Google's mapping, routing, places, and geospatial data to embed interactive maps and location-aware features into web and mobile applications. The platform is organized into four main product families: Maps (dynamic, static, and photorealistic 3D maps; Street View; aerial imagery), Routes (turn-by-turn directions, distance matrix, route optimization for single and multi-stop fleets), Places (place search, autocomplete, place details with reviews, address validation, geocoding, and geolocation), and Environment (air quality, pollen, solar, and weather data). Geospatial Analytics capabilities — including Places Insights, Imagery Insights, and Roads Management Insights — allow enterprises to run location-intelligence queries directly in BigQuery against Google's POI, imagery, and traffic datasets. The platform also integrates with Vertex AI for LLM grounding via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), enabling generative AI applications to reference fresh, real-world geographic data. Pricing is primarily pay-as-you-go per API call, with subscription plans available for predictable pricing on the most popular APIs. A free monthly credit of $200 is applied to each billing account.

### Use Cases
* Interactive maps embedded in consumer-facing web and mobile apps, including custom styling, overlays, and real-time traffic (e.g., store locators, ride-share interfaces)
* Multi-stop fleet route optimization for delivery, logistics, or field-service operations to minimize distance or time across many waypoints
* Address validation and standardization at checkout or sign-up flows to improve delivery accuracy and reduce failed transactions
* Place search and autocomplete to let users quickly find businesses, landmarks, or addresses with rich contextual details (ratings, hours, photos)
* Geospatial analytics in BigQuery — combining internal business data with Google's POI or imagery datasets for site selection, competitive analysis, or infrastructure monitoring
* Grounding generative AI (LLM) responses in live Google Maps data via the Vertex AI integration or Grounding Lite MCP tool for factual, location-aware AI assistants
* Environmental data APIs (air quality, solar irradiance, pollen, weather) for health, sustainability, or energy applications
* Navigation SDK integration into OEM in-car systems or third-party mobile apps that require turn-by-turn guidance with Google Maps' real-time traffic model
